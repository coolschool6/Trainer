Quick Guide

How the coach handles different workout types

Strength days

Use compound lifts, track reps, and push progression when recovery is stable.

Conditioning days

Keep pace repeatable, prefer low-impact options when joints are irritated, and avoid turning every cardio day into a test.

Mobility days

Reduce fatigue, open stiff areas, and prep the body for the next quality session instead of chasing intensity.

Swap Ideas

Examples the coach can use right away

Lower body swap

Replace lunges with step-ups, goblet squats, or split squats when knees need a cleaner pattern.

Short session swap

Convert a 45-minute lifting day into a 20-25 minute high-efficiency circuit without losing the main movement pattern.

5K adjustment

Shift a fat-loss block into intervals, tempo work, and easy aerobic sessions when the goal changes to running faster.
